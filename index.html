Software Modules

Laplace Dome is Modular

The complete platform protects a site end-to-end. The individual modules embed into partner products.

Riemann

AI Detection & Classification Engine

Intelligence engine at the centre of the platform. It ingests multi-source sensor data, detects and classifies aerial objects in real time, and continuously updates its threat assessment as new information arrives.

Proprietary CNN optimised for NVIDIA Orin NX edge deployment.

  • 90%+ hit rate in daylight conditions
  • Object recognition from 30 pixels
  • FPV, loitering munitions, recon UAVs, tactical platforms
  • Two simultaneous streams, low-latency edge inference

Lagrange

Autonomous Flight & Decision Controller

The onboard system that governs every movement and decision of an interceptor UAV in flight. Without external instruction, it calculates the optimal intercept trajectory, adapts to target manoeuvres in real time, and executes the mission from launch to neutralisation.

Handles guidance, sequencing, and terminal manoeuvring — no GPS dependency, no continuous operator input required.

  • Fully autonomous intercept execution
  • Closed-loop integration with Riemann
  • GPS-denied environment operation
  • Compatible with multiple UAV platforms

Itô

Simulation, Training & Validation

A high-fidelity synthetic world in which AI models are built, tested, and certified before any live deployment. It generates adversarial threat scenarios at scale, with realistic sensor noise and unpredictable behaviour, allowing the system to learn from thousands of engagements it has never physically flown.

SITL/HITL validated transfer pipeline ensures simulation performance translates to the field.

  • High-fidelity threat behaviour simulation
  • Risk-free autonomy and multi-target testing
  • SITL/HITL validated live transfer pipeline
  • Rapid iteration against evolving threat profiles

Parseval

Multi-Sensor Intelligence & Data Fusion

The system that ingests simultaneous feeds from radar, acoustic, RF, optical, and other sensor modalities and resolves them into a single, continuously updated operational picture. Where individual sensors see fragments, Parseval sees the whole.

Neural inference and trajectory modelling generate engagement-ready threat profiles for each tracked target.

  • Real-time cross-sensor fusion & validation
  • Predictive trajectory and behaviour modelling
  • Cross-modal correlation reduces false positives
  • Precise coordinates, speed vectors, threat profiles

Laplace

Operational Command & Control Centre

Command and control interface — the operator's window into Laplace Dome. It presents the full operational picture, all active tracks, all threat assessments, and all system states in a single unified display. The operator can monitor, intervene, override, or assume direct control at any point.

Every autonomous decision is observable, every action overridable — operator authority is preserved at every point in the response chain.

  • Real-time visualisation across the full perimeter
  • One-click override at any decision point
  • Configurable rules of engagement per deployment
  • Desktop and iPad deployment
Customer Segments

Who We Protect

01
Airports

Any drone — even a recreational one — entering the approach corridor forces an immediate halt to all landing and takeoff operations. One hour of closure at a busy airport costs millions in grounded aircraft, crew disruption, and passenger compensation. The cost of a single incident routinely exceeds the annual licence fee for a protection system.

02
Military Structures

A $500 FPV drone carrying a small payload can destroy a $2 million vehicle or disable critical base infrastructure. Modern conflict has demonstrated that small commercial drones cause damage far beyond their cost. Bases without autonomous aerial defense are exposed continuously — attackers choose their moment, and human sentries cannot maintain vigilance around the clock.

03
Prisons

Drone-based contraband delivery is now the primary method for getting drugs, phones, and weapons into prisons across Europe. Each successful delivery undermines facility control, enables criminal coordination from inside, and exposes management to regulatory and legal liability. Walls, cameras, and guards have no answer to a drone arriving from above at night.

04
Government & Border Security

Government facilities and border zones are targets because of what they represent — authority, intelligence, and national sovereignty. A surveillance drone over an embassy or a ministry doesn't need to carry a weapon: the intelligence it collects can be worth more than any physical asset. At borders, undetected aerial crossings — whether carrying payloads or mapping sensor gaps — have consequences that compound over time.

05
Energy Infrastructure

A strike on a single high-voltage transformer can black out an entire region for days or weeks — transformers take months to replace and are rarely held in stock. The cost of a prolonged outage, measured in economic disruption, emergency response, and infrastructure repair, runs into the hundreds of millions. Physical strikes on energy infrastructure are immediate and very difficult to reverse.

06
Water Supply

Water supply systems are high-impact, low-awareness targets. A single contamination event at a reservoir can force regional bottled-water distribution for weeks and trigger public-health emergencies that dwarf the cost of the attack itself. Open-air treatment ponds, elevated storage tanks, and remote pumping stations are all accessible from above — and most are protected only against ground-level threats.

07
Ports & Logistics

A major port processes billions in cargo every week. A drone over the port basin can map container positions, cargo schedules, and security patterns in minutes — intelligence that enables theft, smuggling, or a targeted strike. The disruption of a key port, even for 24 hours, cascades through supply chains across entire industries and countries.

08
Gas & Oil

Oil and gas infrastructure spans vast, often remote areas — pipelines, compression stations, refineries, and offshore platforms that cannot all be staffed around the clock. A drone strike doesn't just damage equipment: it triggers environmental incidents, regulatory investigations, and supply disruptions that take months and hundreds of millions to resolve. The remote footprint makes conventional security impractical.

09
Chemical & Industrial Production

A drone strike on a chemical storage or production facility is not just a physical threat — it is a public safety emergency. A toxic release forces mass evacuation, contaminates land and water, triggers regulatory shutdowns, and generates liability exposure that outlasts the physical damage by years. Aerial surveillance of production processes is also a growing vector for industrial espionage.

10
UAV Manufacturers — OEM

Building autonomous AI detection and guidance capability from scratch requires years of development and specialist machine learning engineers — resources most UAV manufacturers do not have. The market for autonomous interceptors is growing fast, and the gap between hardware-only platforms and AI-powered ones is widening. Manufacturers who integrate proven autonomy software ship better products faster.

Most counter-drone systems stop at detection — they alert an operator that a drone is present, and a human decides what to do. Laplace Dome closes the entire loop autonomously. It detects the threat, classifies its type, models its trajectory, dispatches an interceptor, and executes terminal guidance without waiting for manual activation. In a fast-moving threat scenario, the difference between detection-only and autonomous response is the difference between watching the threat arrive and neutralising it before it reaches the perimeter. The operator retains full visibility and override capability throughout.
OSY Fly does not manufacture sensors or UAVs. Laplace Dome is a software intelligence layer that integrates with partner hardware — existing radar installations, third-party camera arrays, and any capable UAV interceptor platform. You are not locked into a proprietary sensor ecosystem, and you can integrate Laplace Dome with infrastructure security systems you already own. It also means we can adapt to new sensor types and effector platforms as the threat landscape evolves, without replacing the core software stack.
No. All critical computation runs on local edge hardware. There is no cloud dependency in the detection, classification, fusion, or guidance pipeline. Infrastructure sites cannot assume communications will be intact during an attack, and any latency introduced by network round-trips is incompatible with the response times required. The system can receive software updates and transmit incident logs when connectivity is available, but these are non-critical functions that do not affect operational performance.
Laplace Dome is designed specifically for GPS-denied and electronically contested environments. Lagrange does not rely on external positioning signals for terminal guidance. Navigation and intercept logic are derived from sensor data processed locally by Riemann and Parseval. This architecture emerged directly from operational experience in active conflict zones where GPS denial and spoofing are routine tactics. It is not an optional resilience feature — it is a core design requirement.
Parseval maintains independent track objects for each detected target simultaneously, each with its own position, speed vector, classification confidence score, and predicted trajectory. Lagrange coordinates multi-intercept sequencing — prioritising threats by type, proximity, and vector — and dispatches available interceptor assets accordingly. A swarm attack scenario involving simultaneous multiple vectors is a design case, not an edge case.
Riemann achieves a verified hit rate exceeding 90% in daylight conditions, with object recognition down to 30 pixels. False positive management is a primary design concern for civil infrastructure deployment. Parseval's cross-sensor validation is the key mechanism: a threat is only escalated to the response layer when confirmed across multiple independent sensor modalities. Classification by Riemann provides a confidence score; Parseval provides cross-sensor corroboration. Both conditions must be met before autonomous response is triggered.

Training data comes from real operational scenarios — including active conflict environments — rather than simulation alone. Laplace Dome integrates with electro-optical cameras, radar, acoustic sensors, and RF monitoring systems. No specific vendor or model is required — the system's hardware-agnostic architecture accepts data from any capable sensor via standard interfaces. For a minimum viable deployment, an EO camera with Riemann provides detection and classification. Adding radar and acoustic inputs through Parseval progressively improves confidence, reduces false positives, and extends effective range.
The Itô simulation and training environment replicates the flight dynamics, sensor signatures, and behavioural patterns of known and novel drone types in a high-fidelity virtual environment. New models are trained in simulation, stress-tested against synthetic adversarial scenarios, and validated via SITL and HITL pipelines before deployment to live systems. This enables OSY Fly to update Riemann's classification capability as new threats emerge — without requiring live-fire testing for each iteration.
